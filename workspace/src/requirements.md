# Staffing and Shift Scheduling Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the staffing requirements and scheduling rules for the Emergency Room (ER) and Intensive Care Unit (ICU). All shift assignments must comply with the department-level and hospital-wide guidelines outlined below. Schedules that do not meet these requirements will not be approved.

## 3. Shift Structure

The daily schedule is divided into three shifts: morning, afternoon, and night. Shift length is determined by department and is specified in the relevant sections below.

---

## 4. Emergency Room (ER)

### 4.1 Shift Length

All ER shifts are **8 hours**.

### 4.2 Staffing Requirements

- Each ER shift must be staffed by a minimum of **2** personnel at all times.
- Staff classified at the **junior** level are not permitted to work the ER night shift.

---

## 5. Intensive Care Unit (ICU)

### 5.1 Shift Length

All ICU shifts are **12 hours**.

### 5.2 Staffing Requirements

- Each ICU shift must be staffed by a minimum of **2** personnel at all times.
- Staff classified at the **trainee** level are not permitted to work any ICU shift.

---

## 6. Hospital-Wide Limits

The following limits apply across both departments. For the purposes of calculating hours worked, each shift assignment counts for the full duration of that department's shift (e.g., one ER shift = 8 hours; one ICU shift = 12 hours).

- No individual may be scheduled for more than **20 hours** total across all departments.
- Total scheduled staff-hours across the entire hospital may not exceed **140 hours**.
